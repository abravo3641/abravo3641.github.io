About me

Hi! I'm Anthony Bravo an upcoming senior majoring in Computer Engineering at the City College of New York. Currently, I am an extension developer intern at Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center where I create and deploy new extensions using Java to aid in the analysis of 4DCT and 4DMRI image scans. Previously I was part of Codepath's iOS university program where I got interested in app development. I carried this interest outside of the program and participated in the Zahn Center Competition where I got to implement the skills I learned to build a tech start-up named Olacare in a team of four. I have recently begun my journey into web development through CUNY Tech Prep and I am looking forward to learning full-stack JavaScript by developing many projects. I am passionate about learning using technology new technology in areas that have not been used before and can greatly benefit from it. Other than learning new technologies I like flying drones, hiking and playing volleyball. You can read more below!

Interests

Drones

Flying drones has been one of the most recent hobbies that I have gotten into. Although most of NYC is labeled as a no-fly zone I still make the effort and travel that extra mile to legally fly my Holy Stone HS 100. I managed to pass the FAA remote pilot exam which lets me fly my drone commercially and use it into to make cool projects.

Volleyball

Despite being Latino and loving to watch soccer I have to say volleyball is by far my favorite sport to play. Ever since playing for my high school team it has stuck with me through my college years. Although I don't play it competitive anymore you can still find me sometimes by the beach courts pretending to be a pro.

Hiking

Whenever I need some time to think or cool off there is not a better activity for me other than hiking. It gives me that peace and quiet that one needs after spending so much time in the city. I recently took a trip to Boulder, Colorado and had one of the best hikes ever by hiking the foothills. It's not everyday one is 5,000 feet above sea level.
